Why PowerDoze exists

Not a romantic story: my own Windows desktop spent most of the day sitting idle at close to full power. I tried the built-in power plans and the sleep timer — they're static settings that don't know what time it is, what's running, or whether I'm even at the desk. The existing tools either just block sleep or just switch power plans; none of them tie together the four things that actually matter — time, running apps, location, and whether you're present — and decide on their own.

I couldn't find it, so I built it. PowerDoze started as the tool I used every day, and only later became a product.
