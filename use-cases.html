UC.01 Gamer

Gaming sessions without interruptions

You're deep into a 3-hour gaming session. Your normal schedule would put the PC to sleep after 30 minutes of "inactivity" (because the game uses a controller, not keyboard). Halfway through, your PC goes dark. Sound familiar?

What you set up

A "Late Night" sleep rule with 5-minute timeout for 00:00–07:00, and add your games to the whitelist.

What PowerDoze does

Detects the game is fullscreen → suspends all sleep rules. You also get an FPS overlay via the hardware monitor.

When you're done

You alt-F4 the game → fullscreen detection ends → "Late Night" rule kicks back in → PC sleeps in 5 minutes.

Zero interruptions during gaming. PC still saves power when you're not playing.
UC.02 Remote Worker

Work from home, all day

You work from home 9–6, with Teams meetings scattered throughout the day. Between meetings, you sometimes step away for 10+ minutes. You want Performance mode during work, Power Saver at night, and zero sleep during calls.

Morning · 09:00

Schedule rule "Work Hours" activates → 60-minute sleep timeout, linked to Balanced mode.

Teams call · 10:30

Meeting app detection sees "ms-teams" in foreground → suspends sleep entirely.

Coffee break · 11:15

Away mode detects 10 minutes idle → switches to Power Saver. You come back → restores Balanced.

Evening · 18:00

"Evening Relax" rule takes over → 30-minute timeout, passive cooling for quiet operation.

Your entire day is automated. Meetings never interrupted. Power saved during breaks and evenings.
UC.03 Student

Campus life on a laptop

You carry your laptop between lectures, the library, and home. Battery life matters. At each location, you want different behavior: Performance for video editing class, Power Saver in the library, Balanced at home.

Wi-Fi · Campus-5G

Wi-Fi geofencing detects campus network → switches to Performance for your creative software class.

Wi-Fi · Library

You move to the library → different SSID → Power Saver kicks in. Battery lasts the whole study session.

Battery below 20%

Battery smart policy triggers → forces Power Saver regardless of location.

Wi-Fi · Home

You get home, plug in → AC power detected → Balanced mode with longer timeouts.

Your laptop adapts to each location automatically. No manual mode switching. Battery survives the full day.
UC.04 Content Creator

Rendering overnight without sleep

You start a 4-hour video render at midnight. Your normal "Late Night" rule would put the PC to sleep in 5 minutes. You need the PC to stay awake for the render, then sleep when it's done.

Before render

Add your video editor (Premiere, DaVinci, Blender) to the app whitelist.

During render

Whitelist detects the editor is running → sleep rules suspended. PC stays awake at full power.

Render finishes

Editor closes → whitelist clears → "Late Night" rule resumes → PC sleeps in 5 minutes.

Render completes uninterrupted. PC sleeps automatically when done. Zero wasted electricity.
UC.05 Office Manager

Track and reduce energy usage

You want to reduce your PC's energy footprint, but you don't know how much power it's actually using or wasting. You need visibility before you can optimize.

Week 1 · Measure

Install PowerDoze with default rules. The analytics dashboard starts tracking mode usage and estimated consumption.

Week 2 · Analyze

Check the energy report: "Your PC was in Performance mode 70% of the time, but only needed it 20%." Identify waste.

Week 3 · Optimize

Add away mode, tighten schedule rules, lower CPU limits in Power Saver mode. Analytics shows the improvement.

Ongoing

Achievement badges motivate continued optimization. CSV export for monthly reports.

Measurable energy reduction with data to prove it. Great for personal goals or company ESG reporting.
UC.06 Home Server / Always-On

Always-on PC, without burning power all night

Your PC runs 24/7 — seeding torrents, serving Plex, recording security cameras, or just staying reachable for remote access. It can never sleep, but it doesn't need full speed at 3 AM either. Around-the-clock full power means wasted electricity, heat, and fan noise.

What you set up

Add your server apps (qBittorrent, Plex, etc.) to the whitelist so the PC never sleeps. Create a "Deep Night" schedule rule for 01:00–08:00 linked to Power Saver mode.

Daytime

Balanced mode keeps the PC responsive for everyday use while your services run in the background.

Night · 01:00

The schedule switches to Power Saver — CPU capped, passive cooling, GPU power limited — but the PC stays awake. Downloads and streams keep running.

End of month

The analytics dashboard shows how many kWh — and how much money — the night hours saved.

Awake 24/7, full power only when needed. Always-on convenience without the always-on electricity bill.
UC.07 Stepping Away

Walk away mid-task without wasting power

You're in the middle of something when the doorbell rings, or it's time to pick up the kids. You don't want to close a dozen windows — so you just walk away, and the PC idles at full speed for half an hour. A few times a day, that waste adds up fast.

You step away

Just leave. No closing windows, no manual mode switching, no thinking about the PC at all.

10 minutes later

Away mode detects no keyboard or mouse input → switches to Power Saver. CPU and GPU power drop while every window stays open.

You come back

Touch the mouse → your previous mode is restored instantly and seamlessly. Meetings and fullscreen video never trigger a false switch.

Get up and go anytime. Zero manual steps, zero idle waste, everything exactly where you left it.
